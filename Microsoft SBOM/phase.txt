Guidebook: Implementing the Microsoft SBOM Tool for Enterprise Monorepos (CERT-In & Client Compliance)
Because your main target is source code—specifically if it involves massive enterprise deployments or mixed-language monorepos—the Microsoft SBOM Tool is a highly scalable, open-source generation alternative
. Unlike tools that scan a folder after the fact, the Microsoft SBOM tool is designed to integrate deeply into your CI/CD pipelines to provide high-performance snapshotting at build time
.
Here is the phased implementation plan to deploy the Microsoft SBOM Tool, ensuring compliance with CERT-In guidelines and fulfilling your client’s 21 mandatory attributes.
Phase 1: START (Foundational Architecture & CI/CD Integration)
In this phase, you lay the groundwork by integrating the tool directly into your development environment
.
Pipeline Integration: Instead of running a manual CLI command on a local folder, embed the Microsoft SBOM Tool directly into your continuous integration and continuous deployment (CI/CD) pipelines
. It leverages Microsoft's internal component detection libraries to natively parse complex environments like NuGet, Go, and pip
.
Format Configuration: Configure the tool to output the inventory in the Software Package Data Exchange (SPDX) format at build time, which is natively supported by the tool and fulfills CERT-In standardization requirements
.
Prepare Post-Processing: Because the Microsoft tool focuses heavily on generating the technical inventory, you must retain the sbomify-action toolkit in your pipeline to automatically query external registries and gather the business data required by your client
.
Phase 2: PROGRESS (Execution, Enrichment, and Attribute Validation)
This phase occurs automatically every time your team builds the source code, fulfilling the requirement to integrate SBOM into each phase of the Secure Software Development Lifecycle (SSDLC)
.
Step 1: Automated Build-Time Generation: As your application compiles, the Microsoft SBOM Tool captures a high-performance snapshot of all source files and dependencies, generating the baseline SPDX document
.
Step 2: Vulnerability Correlation: The Microsoft SBOM Tool specializes in deep dependency cataloging but does not natively scan for vulnerabilities. To fulfill Attribute 8 (Vulnerabilities) and Attribute 9 (Patch Status), you must correlate the resulting SPDX document with a vulnerability scanner or your CI/CD security tools
.
Step 3: Business Data Enrichment: Pass the SPDX document through the sbomify-action toolkit
. This will inject the missing lifecycle data mandated by your client: Release Date (Attribute 10), End-of-Life Date (Attribute 11), Supplier (Attribute 4), and strict License terms (Attribute 5)
.
Step 4: Unique Identifier Standardization: Ensure that the final enriched SBOM assigns Attribute 21 (Unique Identifier) using the exact Package URL (PURL) scheme: pkg:supplier/OrganizationName/ComponentName@Version
.
Phase 3: ADVANCE (Exploitability Management & Secure Delivery)
This final phase ensures that the data is actionable and securely delivered to the client without exposing your proprietary monorepo architecture
.
Step 1: VEX & CSAF Generation: Using the vulnerability data mapped in Phase 2, generate a Vulnerability Exploitability eXchange (VEX) document
. You must classify the vulnerabilities flagged in your components into one of four statuses: Not affected, Affected, Fixed, or Under Investigation
. Follow this with a Common Security Advisory Framework (CSAF) advisory detailing mitigation steps
.
Step 2: Access Control: Because the Microsoft SBOM Tool captures your entire enterprise build environment, the resulting SBOM is highly sensitive
. Split the output into a Public SBOM (for general stakeholders) and a Private SBOM (containing sensitive vulnerabilities or proprietary architectures)
.
Step 3: Attestation and Distribution: Cryptographically sign the final SBOM and VEX documents to ensure integrity and authenticity
. Use secure file-sharing platforms or Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) mechanisms to deliver the final artifacts to your client
.
